# Thinking Types: Character Design Spec
## For illustrator / design team

---

## Design Direction

**Style:** Low-poly geometric illustration (like the MBTI character set), friendly and approachable. All characters are ~11 years old (Grade 5-6). Diverse in gender, ethnicity, and presentation — no type is tied to a demographic.

**Tone:** Every character looks confident, capable, and likeable. No type should look "worse" or "less cool" than another. A student should be able to see any of these characters and think "that one's cool."

**Consistency:** All 8 characters share the same body proportions, line weight, and level of detail. They differ in color palette, costume, props, pose, and expression.

---

## The 8 Characters

---

### 🏛️ The Architect
**Color palette:** Navy blue + silver accents
**Vibe:** Calm confidence. The kid who always has a plan.

| Element | Description |
|---------|------------|
| Costume | Clean-cut outfit — buttoned vest over a shirt, sleeves rolled up. Looks put-together but not stuffy. |
| Props | Holds a rolled-up blueprint in one hand; a geometric compass (the drawing kind) tucked in vest pocket. A small, precise structure (maybe a bridge or tower model) is being built beside them. |
| Pose | Standing upright, one hand on hip, the other holding the blueprint with quiet confidence. Weight evenly distributed — grounded, stable. |
| Expression | Slight, knowing smile. Eyes focused forward — they see the plan clearly. |
| Background element | Faint grid lines behind them — suggesting structure and order. |
| Signature detail | A tiny perfect geometric shape (hexagon or triangle) as a pin or badge on their vest. |

**What the character communicates:** "I think things through. I build things that hold up."

---

### 🔍 The Detective
**Color palette:** Emerald green + warm brown accents
**Vibe:** Curious and careful. The kid who notices what everyone else misses.

| Element | Description |
|---------|------------|
| Costume | Casual layers — a lightweight jacket with lots of pockets (each pocket holds something: a pencil, a folded note, a magnifying glass). Practical, explorer-meets-scholar look. |
| Props | Magnifying glass in one hand, held up near eye level. A thick notebook tucked under the other arm, with colorful tabs sticking out from multiple pages — suggesting many lines of investigation. |
| Pose | Leaning slightly forward, mid-step, as if following a trail. One eyebrow raised. Body language says "wait, let me look closer." |
| Expression | Focused but warm — curious eyes, slight squint of concentration, hint of a smile. Not stern — more like "this is fascinating." |
| Background element | Faint connecting lines and dots behind them — like a detective's evidence board or constellation map. |
| Signature detail | A small question mark motif somewhere on their jacket or notebook — their favorite punctuation. |

**What the character communicates:** "I look at all the angles before I decide."

---

### 🔥 The Advocate
**Color palette:** Coral red + warm gold accents
**Vibe:** Passionate energy. The kid who speaks up.

| Element | Description |
|---------|------------|
| Costume | Expressive outfit — a graphic tee with a bold (abstract) design, an open jacket that flows with movement. Looks like someone who dresses to express, not to conform. |
| Props | One hand raised mid-gesture, as if making a passionate point. The other hand holds a sheet of paper — their written argument, slightly crumpled from enthusiasm. A speech bubble shape floats near them (empty — the point is they HAVE something to say). |
| Pose | Dynamic stance — one foot slightly forward, leaning into the conversation. Weight on front foot, body angled as if addressing an audience. Full of forward momentum. |
| Expression | Bright eyes, open mouth mid-sentence, eyebrows up — pure conviction. Not angry — inspired. |
| Background element | Radiating lines behind them — like a burst of energy or a spotlight. |
| Signature detail | An exclamation mark motif — on a bracelet, or as a small pin. Their favorite punctuation. |

**What the character communicates:** "I believe in this, and I can make you believe it too."

---

### 🧭 The Explorer
**Color palette:** Teal + sunset orange accents
**Vibe:** Creative wonder. The kid who connects things nobody else connects.

| Element | Description |
|---------|------------|
| Costume | Eclectic layers — a mix of patterns and textures that somehow works. A scarf or bandana tied loosely. Looks like someone who picks up interesting things from everywhere they go. |
| Props | Holds a compass in one hand (not pointing any single direction — the needle is spinning). The other hand reaches toward a cluster of floating objects: a book, a leaf, a gear, a star — different domains they're connecting. A small sketchbook pokes out of a back pocket. |
| Pose | Mid-stride, looking up and to the side, as if a new idea just appeared in the air. One hand reaching for it. Body language is open, expansive — they take up space with their curiosity. |
| Expression | Wide eyes, slight open-mouth wonder, the "aha" moment face. Delighted by a new connection they just made. |
| Background element | Dotted curved lines connecting disparate objects — showing the connections they see between things. |
| Signature detail | A tilde (~) or infinity symbol motif — suggesting boundless connections. |

**What the character communicates:** "What if this thing is connected to THAT thing?"

---

### 📋 The Reporter
**Color palette:** Sky blue + clean white accents
**Vibe:** Reliable precision. The kid you trust to get the facts right.

| Element | Description |
|---------|------------|
| Costume | Smart-casual, neat — a polo or clean crew-neck, well-fitted. Everything in order. Looks like a young journalist ready for the field. A press-badge style name tag or lanyard. |
| Props | A notepad held open in one hand, pencil in the other, actively writing something down. The notepad has neat, organized lines of notes visible. A small camera or recorder clipped to their belt — they capture everything. |
| Pose | Standing alert, weight balanced, head slightly tilted as if listening carefully. One hand writing, body slightly turned as if facing a source. Professional but approachable. |
| Expression | Attentive, focused, a small concentrated smile. Eyes locked on what they're observing. The "I'm going to get this exactly right" face. |
| Background element | Clean horizontal lines behind them — like lined notebook paper, suggesting order and accuracy. |
| Signature detail | A period/full-stop motif — they deal in statements and facts, not questions. Precise. |

**What the character communicates:** "I got it. I wrote it down. You can trust my notes."

---

### 📚 The Collector
**Color palette:** Amber gold + deep brown accents
**Vibe:** Enthusiastic gatherer. The kid who always finds one more source.

| Element | Description |
|---------|------------|
| Costume | Layered, practical — a utility vest or cargo jacket with many pockets, each holding something different (a bookmark, a folded article, a pen). A crossbody satchel overflowing with books and papers. Looks like a walking library. |
| Props | Arms full — carrying a stack of books of different sizes and colors, some with bookmarks sticking out. One hand balancing the stack, the other reaching for yet another book from a shelf or pile. Maybe a book is tucked under their chin. |
| Pose | Slightly off-balance from carrying so much — but happy about it. Mid-reach for one more thing. Body language says "I found ANOTHER great source!" |
| Expression | Excited, bright-eyed, slightly overwhelmed but thrilled. The "you won't BELIEVE what I found" face. |
| Background element | Scattered icons behind them — book spines, article snippets, data points — a rich but unorganized field of information. |
| Signature detail | An ampersand (&) motif — there's always one more thing. "This AND this AND this." |

**What the character communicates:** "I found so many great things! Look at all of this!"

---

### ⚡ The Improviser
**Color palette:** Orange + electric yellow accents
**Vibe:** Bold instinct. The kid who jumps in first.

| Element | Description |
|---------|------------|
| Costume | Sporty and energetic — a zip-up hoodie half-unzipped, sneakers that look fast. Everything about them says motion and momentum. A snapback cap worn slightly askew. |
| Props | Minimal — they don't carry a lot because they work from instinct, not notes. One hand snapping fingers or pointing forward, as if saying "I got this." Maybe a single crumpled paper in the other hand — their rough draft, written fast, already done. A small lightning bolt icon on their hoodie or cap. |
| Pose | Dynamic action pose — one foot off the ground, leaning forward, as if they just landed from a jump or are about to take off. Maximum kinetic energy. |
| Expression | Big confident grin, eyes bright, eyebrows up. Not cocky — genuinely stoked. The "let's GO" face. |
| Background element | Speed lines or a small lightning bolt trail behind them — pure momentum. |
| Signature detail | A dash (—) motif — they start a thought and keep moving. Fast, direct. |

**What the character communicates:** "I don't need to overthink it. Watch this."

---

### 🌊 The Wanderer
**Color palette:** Lavender + soft mint accents
**Vibe:** Gentle curiosity. The kid whose mind is always somewhere interesting.

| Element | Description |
|---------|------------|
| Costume | Soft, comfortable layers — an oversized sweater or cardigan, comfortable shoes. Looks like someone who spends time thinking, not performing. A beanie or headband. Dreamy but not sloppy — intentionally cozy. |
| Props | No single prop dominates — instead, they're surrounded by floating idea bubbles: a question mark, a music note, a planet, a paintbrush, a leaf, a math symbol. All the things catching their attention. One hand gently reaching up toward the closest floating idea, as if about to pluck it from the air. |
| Pose | Relaxed, looking upward or into the middle distance. Slight head tilt. One hand reaching toward an idea, the other resting at their side. Not lost — wondering. Body language is open and unhurried. |
| Expression | Soft smile, dreamy eyes, slightly unfocused gaze — looking at something only they can see. Peaceful, not vacant. The "hmm, what if..." face. |
| Background element | Soft, swirling patterns — like gentle waves or aurora lights. Nothing sharp or angular. |
| Signature detail | An ellipsis (...) motif — their thoughts are always continuing, never quite finished. |

**What the character communicates:** "There's so much to think about... what if we looked at it from over here?"

## Character Relationships — Visual Cues

### Color Proximity on the Cube

When all 8 characters appear together (e.g., on a class poster or teacher dashboard), arrange them to show the cube relationships:

```
        🏛️ Navy ────── 🔍 Emerald
        │  ╲              │  ╲
        │   🔥 Coral ──── 🧭 Teal
        │    │            │    │
        📋 Sky ─────── 📚 Amber
           ╲              │  ╲
            ⚡ Orange ─── 🌊 Lavender
```

### Punctuation Motifs at a Glance

Each character has a signature punctuation mark. This is a subtle unifying design element — you're a thinker, your thinking has a rhythm, and this is yours:

| Type | Punctuation | Why |
|------|------------|-----|
| 🏛️ Architect | **.** (period) | Definitive. Statements, not questions. |
| 🔍 Detective | **?** (question mark) | Always asking, always investigating. |
| 🔥 Advocate | **!** (exclamation mark) | Conviction, energy, emphasis. |
| 🧭 Explorer | **~** (tilde) | Connections, approximations, "this relates to that." |
| 📋 Reporter | **:** (colon) | Here are the facts: listed, clear, organized. |
| 📚 Collector | **&** (ampersand) | And this, and this, and this. |
| ⚡ Improviser | **—** (dash) | Quick pivots, bold strokes, forward motion. |
| 🌊 Wanderer | **...** (ellipsis) | Trailing off, continuing, open-ended. |

### Interaction Poses

For materials that show two characters together (e.g., peer review pairing recommendations), design interaction poses for the 4 opposite pairs:

| Pair | Interaction Concept |
|------|-------------------|
| 🏛️ Architect + 🌊 Wanderer | Architect is showing Wanderer a blueprint; Wanderer is pointing at something unexpected on it that the Architect didn't notice. Both look intrigued. |
| 🔍 Detective + ⚡ Improviser | Detective is examining something with their magnifying glass; Improviser has already jumped ahead and is waving them forward. Detective looks amused. |
| 🔥 Advocate + 📚 Collector | Advocate is mid-speech; Collector is holding up a book that supports (or challenges) what they're saying. Both engaged. |
| 🧭 Explorer + 📋 Reporter | Explorer is drawing wild connecting lines on a board; Reporter is translating it into clean notes. They're working together, not in tension. |

## Usage Guidelines

### Where characters appear

| Surface | How Characters Are Used |
|---------|----------------------|
| Teacher dashboard — student card | Small character icon next to the student's type label |
| Teacher dashboard — class view | All 8 characters shown in a legend; students grouped under their current type's character |
| Grouping recommendations | Interaction poses showing suggested pairs |
| Teacher onboarding / PD | Full character introductions with descriptions |
| Print materials / posters | Classroom poster showing all 8 types as a reference |

### Where characters do NOT appear

| Surface | Why Not |
|---------|--------|
| Student-facing screens | Students never see their type label or character. The system gives them tailored feedback without revealing the classification. |
| Parent reports | Parents see dimension scores and narrative descriptions, not type labels. Avoids "my child is a Wanderer, is that bad?" conversations. |
| Formal assessment reports | Types are instructional tools, not assessment outcomes. Keep them out of anything that looks like a report card. |
